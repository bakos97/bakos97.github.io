About Me

A quick snapshot of who I am and what drives me.

I studied at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), where I worked across data science, machine learning, social network analysis, and cognitive modelling. My coursework and projects gave me hands-on experience turning raw data into actionable insight — from interactive visualisations of societal trends to deep-learning pipelines for image segmentation.

I'm drawn to projects with real-world impact: analysing gender disparities in Denmark using public statistics, mapping music communities with graph theory, and automating X-ray image segmentation with neural networks.

I thrive at the intersection of analytics and engineering, and I'm always looking for the next interesting problem to solve.
